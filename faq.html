Car wrapping, answered

Everything you've
wondered about wraps.

Straight answers on cost, durability, paint safety, PPF, tint and aftercare — from the team that wraps cars in Etobicoke every day. Can't find your question? Call or text us.

Cost & value

How much does it cost to wrap a car in Toronto?

A full colour-change wrap starts at $2,599. Price scales with vehicle size, the material you choose and how much coverage you need — a compact coupe costs less than a large SUV, and specialty finishes like chrome or colour-shift cost more than standard gloss. Smaller jobs are far cheaper: a hood wrap is $280, a roof wrap is $300, ceramic window tint starts at $120 and headlight tint starts at $65. We always quote an exact price before any work begins.

Is wrapping cheaper than painting?

Usually, yes. A quality respray that matches factory finish requires stripping, prep, booth time and clear coat — typically several thousand dollars more than a wrap. A wrap also has two advantages paint doesn't: it's reversible, and it protects the original paint underneath.

Does a wrap affect my resale value?

Generally it helps. Because the vinyl shields factory paint from UV and light abrasion, paint under a well-maintained wrap is often in better shape than an unwrapped equivalent. You can remove the wrap before selling and present original paint. A permanent respray usually reduces resale value on most vehicles.

Can I wrap a leased vehicle?

Yes — it's one of the most popular reasons people wrap. The film is removable, so you can run a custom colour for the length of your lease and hand the car back in factory paint. It also shields against minor chips and swirls, which can help you avoid wear-and-tear charges.

Durability & care

How long does a car wrap last?

About 5 to 7 years with quality film and a proper install. Lifespan depends on the material, how the vehicle is stored and how it's washed — garaged and hand-washed cars last noticeably longer. Horizontal panels (hood, roof) take the most UV and usually show wear first.

When can I wash it after installation?

Wait 10 days so the adhesive fully cures and edges lock down. After that, hand wash with a pH-neutral soap. Avoid aiming high-pressure jets straight at seams and edges, and dry with a soft microfibre. We include a complimentary check-up after your wrap.

Can I take a wrapped car through a car wash?

Hand washing is safest. Touchless automatic washes are generally fine, but avoid brush or "soft-touch" tunnels — spinning brushes catch edges and lift film over time. Keep pressure washers at a moderate setting and never aim straight into an edge.

How do I care for a wrap through a Canadian winter?

Rinse road salt off regularly — it's the main enemy of wraps and paint through an Ontario winter. Hand wash rather than brush wash, and never scrape ice directly off wrapped panels; scrapers gouge vinyl far more easily than paint. If a section starts to lift, bring it in early before water and salt get underneath.

Will it fade, peel or bubble?

Quality cast film on properly prepped panels shouldn't bubble or peel within its rated life, and modern films include UV inhibitors that slow fading. Failures almost always trace back to cheap calendered film, poor surface prep, or leaving a wrap on years past its lifespan.

Can a damaged section be repaired?

Yes — a real advantage over paint. Because the car is wrapped panel by panel, a scuffed bumper or scratched door can usually be re-wrapped on its own instead of redoing the whole vehicle. Colour matching is best earlier in the wrap's life.

Paint safety & removal

Will a wrap damage my paint?

No. On healthy factory paint, quality film protects the surface from UV, light scratches and road grime — and comes off cleanly. Problems only arise over failing paint, aftermarket respray or body filler, or when cheap film is left on far past its lifespan. That's why we inspect your paint before quoting.

Can you wrap over damaged paint, rust or dents?

Not reliably. Vinyl needs a smooth, sound surface and will telegraph dents, chips, peeling clear coat and rust rather than hide them. Film over failing paint can also lift that paint on removal. If your paint has issues, get bodywork done first — we'll tell you honestly at consultation.

Can you wrap a freshly painted car?

Let new paint fully cure first — normally around 30 days, though follow your body shop's guidance. Wrapping too early traps solvents still gassing out of the paint, causing bubbling and adhesion failure. Tell us the paint date and we'll plan around it.

Is a wrap removable, and how long does it take?

Yes. Controlled heat softens the adhesive so the film releases cleanly, followed by residue cleanup. Removal typically takes a day or less depending on vehicle size and film age. Wraps left on well past their lifespan take longer to remove cleanly.

Process & timing

How long does an install take?

A full colour change generally takes 3 to 5 days — decontamination, disassembly of trim where needed, the install, then post-heating so the film sets. Window tint, light tint, chrome delete or a hood wrap are often same-day.

What finishes can I choose?

Gloss (closest to factory paint), satin (soft sheen), matte (completely flat), or specialty films like chrome, colour-shift, brushed metal, carbon fibre and textured. Gloss and satin are the most popular for full colour changes. We'll show you real samples on your vehicle before cutting anything.

Is matte harder to maintain than gloss?

Yes, somewhat. Matte and satin can't be polished to remove marks the way gloss can, they show fingerprints and oily residue more readily, and they need matte-specific products — never wax or gloss polish. Gloss is the most forgiving and behaves closest to normal paint.

PPF, coatings & tint

Wrap vs. paint protection film (PPF) — what's the difference?

A wrap is about appearance — it changes colour and finish. PPF is a clear, thicker, self-healing urethane layer built purely to absorb rock chips and scratches while leaving your colour visible. Wrap changes how the car looks; PPF protects how it already looks. Many clients do both.

Should I get PPF, a ceramic coating, or a wrap?

Depends on the goal. Wrap for a different colour or finish. PPF for real physical protection against rock chips, especially the front end for highway driving. Ceramic coating mainly for easier cleaning, water beading and UV resistance — but a coating won't stop a stone chip. PPF plus a coating is the strongest combination.

Is partial PPF worth it, or should I do the full car?

Partial PPF (from $300) covers the zones that actually take damage — front bumper, part of the hood, fenders and mirrors. For most daily drivers that captures the large majority of real-world chip damage at a fraction of the cost. Full-body PPF (from $5,500) makes sense for exotics and high-value vehicles.

What is ceramic window tint and is it worth it?

Ceramic tint uses non-metallic particles to reject infrared heat rather than relying on dye or metal. Versus cheap dyed film it keeps the cabin noticeably cooler, blocks UV that fades your interior, won't interfere with phone/GPS/radio signals, and won't turn purple or bubble. Starts at $120.

What are the window tint rules in Ontario?

Ontario restricts tint that obstructs the driver's view — the front windshield and front side windows are the regulated areas, while rear side windows and the rear windshield are treated more permissively. The wording is based on visibility rather than a single published percentage, and enforcement can vary, so we'll recommend a shade that keeps you on the safe side. Confirm current Highway Traffic Act requirements before choosing a very dark front tint.
